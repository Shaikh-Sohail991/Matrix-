Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 2025. We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience on our website. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your preferences. By continuing to use our site, you consent to our use of cookies as described below. For more on how we handle personal data, see our Privacy Policy.

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, tablet, or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to site owners. Cookies can be "first-party" (set by us) or "third-party" (set by another domain, such as an analytics provider). They may also be "session" cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent" cookies (stored for a set period or until you delete them).

Other Similar Technologies

We may also use technologies similar to cookies, such as:

  • Local storage: Data stored in your browser that persists after you close it (e.g., for saving preferences).
  • Session storage: Temporary data that is cleared when you close the tab or browser.
  • Pixels and beacons: Small graphics used to track whether emails or pages have been opened and to measure usage.

In this policy, we use "cookies" to refer to cookies and these similar technologies where relevant.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly necessary cookies: These are essential for the website to function. They enable basic features like page navigation, secure areas, and load balancing. The site cannot operate properly without them, and they generally do not store personally identifiable information. You cannot opt out of these through our site, but you can block them in your browser (some site features may then not work).
  • Functional cookies: These allow the website to remember choices you make (e.g., language, region, username) and to provide enhanced or personalized features. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we use.
  • Analytics and performance cookies: These help us understand how visitors use our website (e.g., which pages are visited most, how long users stay, and whether they see error messages). The data is usually aggregated and anonymous. We use this information to improve our site and services.
  • Advertising or targeting cookies: These may be set by us or our advertising partners to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant content or ads on our site or on other sites. They may also be used to limit how often you see an ad and to measure the effectiveness of campaigns. You can opt out of interest-based advertising through your browser or device settings, or through industry tools where available.

Specific Cookies We Use

We may use cookies from the following categories on our site. The exact list may change as we update our services; we will keep this policy updated to reflect current practice.

  • Site functionality: Session and preference cookies for navigation, forms, and language/region settings.
  • Analytics: Cookies from analytics providers to measure traffic, behavior, and performance (e.g., page views, bounce rate). Data is used in aggregate form.
  • Marketing (if applicable): Cookies used to deliver relevant offers and to measure campaign performance, where we run advertising or remarketing.

Your Choices

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers let you refuse or accept cookies, or delete existing cookies. You can usually find these options under "Settings," "Privacy," or "Cookies." Blocking all cookies may prevent some parts of our site from working correctly.
  • Opt-out tools: For analytics or advertising cookies, you may be able to use industry opt-out tools (e.g., Network Advertising Initiative or similar, depending on your region).
  • Do Not Track: Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" signal. We may take such signals into account where technically and legally supported; however, there is no universal standard, so we also rely on the choices you make in your browser and on our site.

If you disable or delete cookies, you may need to reset your preferences on future visits, and some features may not work as intended.

Duration of Cookies

Session cookies are removed when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain for a set period (e.g., from a few days to several years) or until you delete them. The retention period depends on the purpose of the cookie; we do not keep cookies longer than necessary for the stated purpose.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, or legal requirements. We will post the revised policy on this page and update the "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
