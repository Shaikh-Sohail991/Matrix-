DC Power Supply

+
1.What is a DC power supply?

A DC (Direct Current) power supply is an electronic device that converts AC mains electricity into a regulated, stable DC voltage and current output. It is used to power electronic devices, test equipment, and components that require a constant, low-noise DC source. DC power supplies are widely used in R&D labs, manufacturing, and field testing environments.

+
2.What is an adjustable power supply?

An adjustable power supply allows users to set and vary both the output voltage and current within a specified range. Unlike fixed power supplies, adjustable models provide greater flexibility for testing different devices and components with varying power requirements.

+
3.What is a programmable DC power supply?

A programmable DC power supply can be controlled and configured via software or a digital interface (such as USB, RS232, LAN, or GPIB). Users can set precise voltage and current values, create automated test sequences, and monitor output remotely — making it ideal for automated test environments (ATE).
